Mixed Reality App to Read Air Quality

A spatial companion that reveals the invisible data on air quality in real time and in precise locations.

Role

Concept, Direction, Technical Lead

Year

2026

Tools

Unity / Unreal, TouchDesigner, custom shaders

Overview

Portal Room is a spatial instrument: a dense, responsive field of light and sound that visitors can lean into, push against, and get lost inside. It explores how XR tools can be used to build presence without headsets—treating the room itself as the headset.

The installation was designed for slow attention: visitors are invited to linger, to notice how small movements ripple through the system, and to feel the space gradually re-tune itself around them.

Context

  • Exhibited as part of an immersive art festival.
  • Room-scale: ~6m x 6m, fully darkened, with a single entrance.
  • Built to run unattended for multi-week engagement.

Interaction & System

Visitors stand within a responsive volume of light. Depth cameras and spatial audio capture movement, which is translated into a fluid, almost liquid response: light fields bend, lines shear, and sound scatters and converges around them.

Instead of discrete “triggers”, the system is continuous—there is never a moment where nothing is happening. The goal is to create a feeling of being inside a living, breathing diagram.

Process highlights

  • Rapid prototyping with realtime engines and custom GLSL shaders.
  • Material studies exploring how haze, projection, and LED interact.
  • On-site tuning sessions to balance latency, brightness, and comfort.
